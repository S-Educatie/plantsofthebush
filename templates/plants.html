Plants of the Bush

The Bush exhibit attempts to replicate a natural rainforest with its ~1500 species of tropical plants from Africa, Asia, and South America. It is successful in replicating the feeling of being a forest through careful management by the staff. However, since it is ultimately a controlled environment, the Bush exhibit is bound to be different from natural rainforests.

The animals and plants in the Bush exhibit are likely to behave differently than their natural environments. Animals may have different daily activity patterns, while plants may flower and fruit at different times than expected. The possible differences are numerous, but knowing all of these is not essential to our work at Burgers' Zoo.

For now, we are interested in the flowering and fruiting patterns of a few plant species in the Bush exhibit. We (and by that, I mean you) will be collecting data for these plant species beyond 2022 for a few years. These data will include the the flowering and fruiting stages of plants at any given point in time, along with the amount of flowers and fruits, and any interesting comments.
